Literally About Letters – Kafe Kultura Installation

The space for dialog between the meanings of the Letter and the Font.

For several months in Kafe Kultura one could observe unique letters and fonts, as well as examples of using of fonts in urban environment, signs, license plates. This installation became another alternation of the Café’s contextual environment. The problems built around the conflict between a letter as a symbol from the alphabet and a letter as a graphical representation of a sound with the help of a font.

The visitors could play with letters, create non-existent words from them. They could take letters from known words and compose new ones, the ones that they could only dream of. There were several font objects in the café.

A calligraphic font by Constantine Selikhanov from Logoses / Wall series. His work translates a saying into a physical sculpture, located along two sides of a wall. Neon inscriptions “Know the price” and “You can be free” represent two poles, and an observer’s objective is to build a connection between them. In this project the author makes references to the Bruce Nauman neon art.

The workshop of Pavel Semchenko exhibited 3 letters manufactured for “Ploshchad Lenina” sign in the Minsk subway system.

A poster by Yuri Toreev for play “Chichikov” of the Kupala Theatre based on “Dead Souls” by Gogol represented a letter as a part of a drawing , mile stone “Ч”.

The café also exhibited several font versions of letter “A” developed for signs in Germany and Portugal, a contribution from the private collection of Taras Kalyagin. US license plates represented the most versatile method of combination of different fonts in a limited space.

The project’s idea belongs to Denis Serebryakov.

The project was implemented by Alexei Shinkarenko.

The café exhibited the works by Pavel Semchenko, Yuri Toreev, Constantine Selikhanov, as well as items from the collection of Taras Kalyagin.
